# BoardCost User Guide

This guide is for account managers and sales staff who want to produce an indicative price for a corrugated POS display while the customer is still on the phone. No technical knowledge is needed.

## Opening the app

If your team has the hosted version, open the BoardCost link in any modern browser (Chrome, Edge, Firefox or Safari, on desktop or mobile). If you have been given the files instead, double-click `index.html`.

## Producing a quote

1. **Choose the display type.** FSDU for full-height floor units, CDU for counter-top units, standee for flat printed stands, dump bin for open bins. The type matters because it changes how much board the unit actually uses. An FSDU with shelves consumes far more board than a standee with the same footprint.
2. **Enter the dimensions** in millimetres: overall width, height and depth of the finished unit. Anything from 50mm to 2400mm per side is accepted. If a number is out of range the app tells you exactly which one and why, in the red box under the button.
3. **Pick the board grade.** E-flute for lightweight counter units, B-flute as the general-purpose default, EB or BC double wall for heavy or long-life displays.
4. **Pick the print process.** Digital suits short runs (low setup, higher per-unit cost); litho-laminated suits long runs of high-graphics work; direct flexo suits simple designs at volume. If you're unsure which is cheapest, just try both, and the quote updates instantly.
5. **Choose finishing.** Gloss or matt lamination, and tick die-cutting if the design has cut-outs or shaped edges. Tick or untick **Include VAT** depending on whether the customer wants figures inc. or ex. VAT.
6. **Enter the quantity** and press **Calculate quote** (or just press Enter).

## Reading the estimate

The estimate panel is laid out like one of our printed job tickets, top to bottom: materials, printing, finishing and assembly add up to the **production cost**; margin is added; any quantity discount is taken off; then VAT is applied to give the **grand total** and the headline **unit price**.

The **price breaks** table underneath shows the ex-VAT unit price at 50, 100, 250, 500 and 1,000 units, with the customer's current tier highlighted. This is the upsell view: if a customer at 100 units can see the unit price at 250, the conversation about a bigger run starts itself.

## Exporting the quote

Press **Export quote as CSV** to download the full breakdown as a dated spreadsheet file (for example `boardcost-quote-2026-07-02.csv`). It opens directly in Excel and can be attached to a customer email or pasted into the estimating workbook.

## Important caveat

BoardCost uses **indicative estimating rates**. It exists to give a fast, sensible ballpark. Final pricing is always confirmed by the estimating team, who account for current board prices, machine loading and delivery.
